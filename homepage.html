STUDIO GHIBLI MOVIES
Howl's Moving Castle is a breathtaking animated fantasy film directed by Hayao Miyazaki and produced by Studio Ghibli, based on Diana Wynne Jones' novel of the same name. The story follows Sophie, a humble young hat-maker who is transformed into an old woman by a curse from the Witch of the Waste. Determined to break the spell, .....Sophie seeks refuge in the moving castle of Howl, a charming but enigmatic wizard with a reputation for both brilliance and cowardice. The castle itself is a marvel of steampunk design—a clanking, wheezing, mechanical beast powered by the fire demon Calcifer, who is bound to Howl by a mysterious contract. Inside, Sophie befriends Markl, Howl’s young apprentice, and gradually unravels the secrets of Howl’s dual life as both a powerful sorcerer and a man desperate to escape his own demons.
Spirited Away is a 2001 Japanese animated fantasy film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki and produced by Studio Ghibli. The story follows Chihiro, a ten-year-old girl who, while moving to a new neighborhood with her parents, accidentally stumbles into a mysterious spirit world......When her parents are transformed into pigs by eating enchanted food, Chihiro must find a way to survive in this strange realm. She gets a job at a magical bathhouse run by the sorceress Yubaba, where she meets unusual creatures and spirits, including the enigmatic Haku.As she learns to be brave, resourceful, and compassionate, Chihiro tries to break the spell on her parents and find a way back to the human world. The film blends traditional Japanese folklore with deep emotional themes of growth, identity, and environmental respect.
Whisper of the Heart is a 1995 Japanese animated romantic drama film directed by Yoshifumi Kondō and written by Hayao Miyazaki, produced by Studio Ghibli.The story centers on Shizuku Tsukishima, a curious and book-loving 14-year-old girl living in Tokyo. One day, she notices that all the library books she borrows have already been checked out by someone named Seiji Amasawa. Intrigued,.....she sets out to discover who he is — leading her into an unexpected friendship and, eventually, a gentle romance.Seiji dreams of becoming a master violin maker and plans to study in Italy, while Shizuku is inspired to discover her own creative path as a writer. As their bond deepens, the film beautifully explores themes of young love, personal growth, artistic ambition, and the courage to pursue your dreams.
Princess Mononoke is a 1997 Japanese animated epic historical fantasy film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki and produced by Studio Ghibli.Set in a mystical version of Japan’s Muromachi period, the story follows Ashitaka, a young prince cursed by a demon while defending his village. Seeking a cure, he journeys west and becomes entangled in a fierce conflict between the forces of industrialization and the gods of the forest. .....At the heart of this struggle is San, a fierce human girl raised by wolves, known as Princess Mononoke, who fights to protect the forest from destruction. She clashes with Lady Eboshi, the ambitious leader of Iron Town, who seeks to expand her industry and provide a better life for outcasts, even at the cost of nature.
Castle in the Sky is a 1986 Japanese animated fantasy-adventure film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki, and produced by Studio Ghibli—the studio’s first official feature.The film follows Sheeta, a mysterious girl who falls from the sky, protected by a magical crystal. She is rescued by Pazu, a brave young boy who dreams of finding the legendary floating city of Laputa. Together, they are pursued by sky pirates, government agents, and a secret military force who want the power of Laputa for themselves......As Sheeta and Pazu uncover the truth about her heritage and the lost city, they discover Laputa is not just a place of beauty and ancient wisdom, but also a dangerous relic of a forgotten age of technological might. Their journey becomes a battle to protect the future from those who would misuse the past.
From Up on Poppy Hill is a 2011 Japanese animated romantic drama film directed by Gorō Miyazaki and co-written by Hayao Miyazaki, produced by Studio Ghibli.Set in 1963 Yokohama, the story follows Umi Matsuzaki, a responsible and thoughtful high school girl who raises signal flags each morning in memory of her father, lost at sea during the Korean War. While managing her family’s boarding house, she meets Shun Kazama, an outspoken student who writes for the school newspaper. .....As Umi and Shun grow closer while working to save their school’s historic clubhouse—Quartier Latin—from demolition, they uncover a tangled family secret that threatens to separate them.
